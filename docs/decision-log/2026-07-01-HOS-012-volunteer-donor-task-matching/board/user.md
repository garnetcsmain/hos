# User Review: HOS-2026-012

## My Recommendation

🟡 RESHAPE

(Specifically: **green-light the three-options-with-reasons UI pattern — it's the right shape, because it's the same shape as the matcher I already trust. Kill "location-weighted to maximize speed" as currently worded, and treat the skill/needs profile as throwaway daily state, not a durable record. The donor piece needs a front door that doesn't assume an organization exists.**)

## User Scenario

I am still **Yolanda**, 44, the parish-refugio volunteer in **Petare**, same cracked-screen Tecno Spark, same rationed Movistar data. Since HOS-2026-010 I have a device-key account and a PIN. It's a Tuesday, not even a crisis night — just the ordinary grind. My cousin's car (which I've been borrowing to run supply routes because mine has been dead for two weeks) needs gas I can't afford this week. I am, today, not a driver. I'm someone who can cook, or fold laundry, or sit with the six-year-old who won't say his name. Yesterday I was a driver. This is the actual texture of the thing this proposal calls "switchable skills."

Alongside me, imagine **Don Ramón**, who owns a small ferretería (hardware store) three blocks from the refugio. He isn't with an NGO, isn't a "responder," has never heard the word "orgId." He has a truck that runs and, this week, no diesel to put in it. He wants to say "I have a truck, no fuel, someone please match me with whoever has fuel and needs a truck" — and then get back to his shop.

**What I need in the next 10 minutes**, in both cases: say what's true about me right now, and either get matched to something I can actually do, or get left alone until something fits. What I don't need: a screen that makes either of us feel evaluated as a person.

## Does This Solve It?

- **Pain addressed: PARTIAL.** The coordinator's pain (who do I call at 11pm — scroll a list, or start dialing?) is real and this fixes it well, *if* built the way `rankOffersForNeed` already works: scored, capped, with reasons, never auto-committing. My pain (say what's true about me, without it becoming paperwork or a permanent record) is only partially addressed — the proposal talks about a "skill model" and a "personal needs model" like they're profile fields to fill in once, and that is not how my Tuesday actually goes.
- **Would I use it: MAYBE, leaning YES for the "three concrete options" pattern, leaning NO for a skill/needs *profile* as durably designed today.** I'll use "here's my situation right now" if it's as fast as texting a friend. I will not maintain a profile page. Nobody in Petare maintains a profile page for anything, including their own bank.

## Friction Points

1. **"Switchable skills" sounds like a settings toggle. It is actually "what am I, right now, mid-shift, before I forget to say so."** → If this is a field I have to remember to go update — open the app, find my profile, tap "driver" off, tap "cook" on — it will go stale the way every optional profile field on every app I've ever used goes stale. My car broke down two weeks ago; if "driver" is still checked because updating it wasn't the thing directly in front of me when the car died, I will get offered a driving task I can't do, waste a coordinator's trust, and the very next thing that happens is the coordinator calls me *by phone* instead of trusting the app, and we're back to where we started. → The update has to piggyback on something I'm already doing, not be its own errand. Concretely: ask me my current skill/need **at the moment I'm asked to act** — when a task comes in, the app can ask "¿Puedes hacer esto ahora? Sí / No, pero puedo con esto otro: [ ]" (Can you do this now? Yes / No, but I could do this instead) — a one-tap correction *in the moment of being asked*, not a profile page I maintain on my own initiative. A day-old "driver" flag that gets silently double-checked at match time is honest; a day-old flag trusted blindly is a liability wearing a green checkmark.

2. **"I have a car but no gas" — who sees this, and does saying it feel like admitting I've failed at something?** This is the one that actually worried me most reading the proposal. There's a real difference between "I'm offering my car" (feels generous, feels like showing up) and "I need gas money/fuel" (feels like admitting I can't afford to keep helping). If my "personal need" is visible the same way my "skill" is — sitting on a profile a coordinator browses — then every time I report a need, I'm broadcasting a small personal failure next to my name, semi-permanently, to whoever's looking. → This needs to feel like *asking a favor of the group*, not *filing a limitation on my file*. Two things would fix that: (a) needs should look and read exactly like the org `Offer`/`Need` pattern already does — a coarse, anonymous-ish "necesidad: combustible, distrito: Petare" that a donor can see and answer, not "Yolanda needs gas" pinned to my name where every coordinator scrolling volunteers sees it forever; (b) it should expire on its own — the moment someone brings me gas, or a few days pass, it should vanish, not sit there as a fact about me that outlives the day it was true.

3. **Being one of "exactly three" feels fine if I can see why — it feels awful if I can't.** I already lived through the version of this that works: `rankOffersForNeed` shows a score AND a plain-language reason list ("Categoría coincide," "Mismo distrito," "Cubre parcialmente"). That's not being ranked as a person — that's a coordinator being handed a cheat sheet. What would feel bad, and what the proposal doesn't yet promise, is a black-box "you're #2 of 3" with no reason shown — because then when I'm passed over (or when I'm picked and it's a bad fit), I have no way to understand it, correct it, or trust it next time. And it cuts both ways: if I ask *why* I wasn't picked and there's no answer, it reads as "the algorithm judged you and won't say why" — the exact opposite of the "AI recommends, people decide" promise this project has kept everywhere else. → Every one of the three options, for me as much as for the coordinator, needs the same visible reasons list the existing matcher already has. Don't build a fancier engine that produces a worse, less explainable UI than the one you've already got.

4. **"Heavily weighted by location to maximize speed" is the line that should scare this board, and it should scare me too, and it doesn't say who's being weighed.** For an org's `Offer`, "same district" is a fact about a warehouse — a building doesn't get followed home. For *me*, "same district, maximize speed" means the system wants to know, with more precision than a district, where I am right now, today, this hour — because a district-level guess is exactly what today's model already has, and if that were precise enough this proposal wouldn't need to ask for more. That's not a hypothetical for someone living where I live. If the price of getting matched faster is the app learning my actual movement pattern — not just "Petare" but which corner of Petare, at which hour, day after day — that's a live risk to me, a volunteer with no army and no lawyer, in a country where a phone in the wrong hands has already gotten people in trouble. → Keep it at `district`, the same coarseness the org side already lives with, and if speed genuinely requires finer precision, that finer signal should be **ephemeral and used once for the match, then thrown away** — never stored as a history of where-Yolanda-has-been. I will accept "the app is a little slower to match me" every single time over "the app now has a diary of my movements."

## Missing Piece — Don Ramón's side

The proposal writes the donor piece as if a "logistics issue" or "request for help" is a form field away from an org's `Need`/`Offer` — but Don Ramón has no `orgId`, no dashboard habit, no time, and honestly no trust yet that this is worth his five minutes. What he has is a phone and a problem. If donor onboarding looks anything like the coordinator console — accounts, categories, districts, a form with fields for things he doesn't have a mental model for — he will do what he already does today: post it on a WhatsApp group and hope a neighbor sees it. The single most important thing the donor flow can get right is **matching the effort of "I have a truck, no fuel" to the effort of literally texting that sentence to a friend** — one line of plain language in, immediately useful in return (matched to a fuel offer, or "necesidad registrada, te avisamos" — need registered, we'll let you know), no login wall up front. If Don Ramón has to make an account before the system will even *listen*, he's gone, and so is his truck.

And a second, smaller thing: Don Ramón is not a "responder" and not a "volunteer" and definitely doesn't want to be asked to pick a skill from a dropdown built for people who show up in person day after day. He wants to give what he already has once or twice, not enroll in a program. Whatever "donor" ends up meaning in the data model, from his chair it needs to feel like *one favor*, not *joining something*.

## Confidence Score

**0.74** — solid, because the shape I'm endorsing (score + visible reasons, never auto-committing) isn't hypothetical to me — it's the matcher this project already built and I already reviewed favorably in spirit. Not higher, for three honest reasons. **One:** I can't see the actual screen where a volunteer would update their skill/need mid-shift, and this whole review turns on whether that's a one-tap in-context correction or a profile page I'm expected to remember to visit — the same "show me the actual screens" caveat I raised on HOS-2026-010 and it's just as true here. **Two:** I genuinely don't know how fine "location-weighted" is meant to get in practice, and the proposal's own text ("must be location based to maximize speed") reads to me like it wants more than `district`, which is the one line I'd fight hardest against. **Three:** Don Ramón is a persona I'm reasoning about, not one I've stood next to the way I've stood next to Yolanda through three prior reviews — my confidence on the donor-onboarding-friction point is real but a notch more inferred than lived.
