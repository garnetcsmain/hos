# Principals Review: HOS-2026-010

## My Recommendation

**RESHAPE** — split the decision and invert its defaults.

The proposal bundles two independent things with opposite risk profiles under one vote. Freshness/attribution of field data is a real, legitimate need. But the design reaches for the single highest-consequence, least-reversible mechanism available (a centralized biometric registry of the volunteer corps in an authoritarian state) as a *co-equal option* alongside a strictly simpler one that meets the same stated goal. Under this project's own principles — data-minimization, crisis-grade & reversible, and "information before interfaces" — that is not a coin-flip. The face-enrollment path is not GREEN, and it is not KILL either: it is **shaped down to a documented last resort that must clear its own separately-justified necessity test.** The geolocation half is **RESHAPE**, not GREEN, because the proposal mis-describes what exists to build it on.

Concretely, the shape I would ratify:
- **D8a (identity / attribution):** GREEN LIGHT for the **non-biometric-alternative** (device-bound keypair + PIN/passphrase, riding on the HOS-2026-001-08 account work) as the *default and only sanctioned v1 path*. Face enrollment is **RESHAPED to KILL-unless-justified**: it may not be built until a written necessity/proportionality memo shows the keypair path *demonstrably fails* against a named threat the registry uniquely solves. Occam plus data-minimization plus irreversibility all point the same way here, and they are three of this project's six stated principles.
- **D8b (geolocation proximity):** RESHAPE. The load-bearing build-on-it claim is factually wrong (see F1), the attestation gap is real and named, and the mechanism is only defensible if it is *advisory metadata attached to a submission*, never a gate — which the proposal says, and which I hold it to.

**Net:** the non-biometric path is ALIGNED or better on all six. The face path is VIOLATED on four of six — the four that matter most for this population and this state. That asymmetry is the whole finding.

## Logic Check

**F1 — "reuse existing VE-CCS-style zone-grid in apps/web/app/lib/geotag.ts" — DOES NOT HOLD. The file does not exist; the capability does not exist.**
I checked. There is no `geotag.ts` anywhere in the repo (`find … -iname '*geotag*'` → nothing), no `tagLocation()` symbol (`grep -rn tagLocation` → nothing), and the *only* occurrence of the string "VE-CCS" in the entire tree is inside this proposal's own `proposal.yaml`. What actually exists for "location" is `apps/web/app/lib/matching/engine.ts:38-46,62-70`: a `locationOverlap()` that does **Jaccard token overlap on free-text city+place strings** — no latitude, no longitude, no zones, no distance, no proximity math. The data model carries `foundLocation`/`lastSeenLocation` as free-text (confirmed in `intake.ts:39,81`); nothing in the app captures a device lat/lng today. So the geofencing half is not "reuse an existing zone-grid" — it is **build a coordinate-capture pipeline, a zone model, and a server-side proximity engine from zero.** This is a materially larger and riskier scope than the proposal (and the "VERIFIED CODEBASE FACTS" header handed to me) claims, and it is a non-sequitur to cost/justify D8b on a building block that isn't there. *If I'm wrong:* if `geotag.ts` exists on an unmerged branch (HOS-2026-001 has a `07-postgres-supabase-adapter.yaml` that mentions pgvector but nothing geospatial), the scope claim is still false *for the branch the Board is reviewing*, and the proposal must name the dependency, not assert the file.

**F2 — "face enrollment is necessary to get trustworthy field data" — DOES NOT HOLD; it is assumed, not entailed.**
The goal (context.problem) is: know a submission "came from who it claims to, from near where it claims." A device-bound keypair signature proves origin ("this came from the enrolled volunteer's provisioned device") *and* a PIN proves a human unlocked it. That satisfies the stated goal. Face recognition adds one marginal property over the keypair: resistance to *device sharing/handoff* (proposal's own words for the alternative's weakness). So the real, narrow claim the face path must defend is not "we need identity assurance" — it's "**unattributed device-sharing among our own vetted volunteers is a threat severe enough to justify collecting permanent biometric PII of the entire corps in Venezuela 2026.**" That is a strong claim and the proposal does not make it; it lists face-recognition first as the "fastest to stand up" and treats the alternative as merely "weaker against device sharing." Fastest-to-stand-up is not a principle; data-minimization is. The logical gap: *convenience and marginal robustness are being traded against irreversibility and a targeting-list risk the proposal itself rates as HIGHER than HOS-006's.* That trade is not entailed by the goal.

**F3 — "geolocation proximity keeps field data fresh and attributable" — HOLDS ONLY AS ADVISORY METADATA, NOT AS AN INTEGRITY CHECK.**
The proposal is honest in its own risk list ("Geolocation on a pure web app is spoofable; no Play Integrity/App Attest-grade attestation without a native app"). Given that, the claim "the device must be physically near … not just anywhere" cannot be *enforced* — a mock-location tool defeats it, and the honest external research on GPS spoofing confirms mock-location is trivially available. So the true entailment is weaker than the summary states: proximity is a **soft signal that raises or lowers coordinator confidence**, not a check that establishes physical presence. Fine — but then it must be *presented* as "device-reported location, unverified," never as "confirmed on-site," or it breaches trust & honesty (the HOS-002 scar: don't record that something happened when it didn't).

**F4 — "always a flag for human review, never an automatic block" — HOLDS, and is the clause the whole proposal rests on.**
This is entailed and correct, and it aligns with "AI recommends, people decide" and with HOS-002's ratified rule that automated/probabilistic signals may *demote/flag* but never override human judgment. My only logic note: the proposal states this as intent in three places but the failure mode is *drift* — the first time a coordinator is overloaded and someone wires "auto-reject failed-geo submissions" to cut queue volume, the principle is silently inverted (exactly how HOS-002's fail-open and fake-"sent" bugs happened). The claim holds *as written*; it needs an enforced invariant, not a comment.

**F5 — "false lockout risk … compounded by demographic bias" — HOLDS, and cuts against the proposal's own goal.**
The proposal names this. Logically it is worse than named: the population is "low digital literacy, low-end phones," and face models are documented to have higher false-reject rates on exactly the demographics (darker skin, women, older devices/cameras) plausibly over-represented in a Venezuelan volunteer corps. So the mechanism intended to *include and trust* the volunteer network has its failure mass concentrated on the network. A fallback lane (in scope) mitigates but does not erase this — if the fallback is the common path, the biometric layer earned its risk for nothing.

**F6 — "coordinators EXEMPT from geofencing" — HOLDS as reasonable, but note the asymmetry it creates.**
Sound rationale (command post vs. field). Logic-consistent. One flag: this means the *only* people whose movements are continuously logged are the field volunteers — the lower-power, higher-risk population — while the higher-power role is untracked. In a surveillance-risk frame that is the wrong asymmetry to bake in silently; it should be an explicit, documented choice with retention limits, not an incidental consequence of an exemption.

## Simplest Solution

**Occam's razor decisively prefers the non-biometric-alternative for D8a, and it is not close.**

The end goal is "a returning, known volunteer signs their submission." The minimum mechanism that achieves that is: a per-volunteer account (already being built in HOS-2026-001-08), a device-bound keypair provisioned at enrollment, and a PIN/passphrase to unlock signing. That is standard, offline-friendly, low-end-phone-friendly, recoverable (reissue on loss), and collects **zero biometric PII.** It satisfies AI-recommends/people-decide (deterministic), information-before-interfaces (no camera UX to build), data-minimization (a public key is not special-category data), auditability (signed events), and crisis-grade/reversible (rotate a lost key) — five of six principles, cleanly.

**What is lost by simplifying:** resistance to a vetted volunteer *handing their unlocked device + PIN to someone else*. That is the entire delta. Against that single lost property you would be buying: a permanent, non-rotatable, special-category biometric database of your own responders; a ready-made "who has been helping" target list the proposal itself rates as a higher-value target than HOS-006; a coercion/forced-unlock exposure; a demographic-bias lockout surface on the population you depend on; a 6–10 week build; a DPA/legal special-category-data burden; and (per UNHCR/Rohingya precedent in the research) a class of system that has *already*, in humanitarian practice, leaked to the government the protected population fled. **Occam plus data-minimization plus reversibility all say: do not pay that for device-sharing resistance among vetted volunteers.** If device-sharing is genuinely the threat, cheaper mitigations exist (short signing-key TTL, re-PIN on each session, per-submission challenge, coordinator-side anomaly flags) — none of which require biometrics.

For **D8b**, the simplest defensible form is: capture coarse device location at submit, compute nearest-zone once a real zone model exists, attach an advisory "in/near assigned zone: yes/no/unknown" flag to the submission event, retain only the flag + coarse zone (not the raw track), and surface it to coordinators as unverified. Anything more (raw lat/lng history, hard gates, spoofing "hardening" on a web app) is either non-crisis-grade or a movement-surveillance dataset, and buys little because the substrate is spoofable anyway.

## Technical Debt Created

- **Phantom-dependency debt (D8b), unacknowledged.** The proposal costs geofencing at "2-3 weeks … reuse existing … geotag.ts." That file/capability does not exist (F1). The real debt is a full geospatial subsystem: coordinate capture, a zone model (the VE-CCS grid is aspirational, not present), a proximity engine, and spoof-labeling. The estimate and the "reuse" framing under-own this. **Owned?** No — it is mis-stated as already-built.
- **Irreversible-PII debt (D8a face path), partially acknowledged.** A biometric registry cannot be "paid down." Deletion policy reduces *retention* but every enrolled template is a standing liability for as long as it exists, and compromise is unrecoverable (can't rotate a face). The proposal names the risk but treats it as manageable via policy; first-principles, this is debt you can never refactor away, only avoid taking on. **Owned?** Named, but understated as a policy problem rather than a permanent liability.
- **Principle-drift debt (both), unacknowledged.** "Advisory, never a block" (F4) is enforced today only by prose. Without a coded invariant (and a test that fails if a geo/face signal ever hard-rejects), the next capacity crunch silently converts advisory→gate. HOS-002 is the precedent: fail-open auth and hard-coded "sent" both entered as reasonable-looking shortcuts. **Owned?** No test or invariant proposed; only intent.
- **Cross-linking / function-creep debt (both), acknowledged.** Good: the proposal explicitly bars linking this registry to the HOS-006 missing/found DB and bars auto-penalties. Hold that line in code (separate stores, separate access policies), because the pgvector `match_embeddings` table provisioned for HOS-006 is a tempting shared substrate that would silently merge the two. **Owned?** Yes, and correctly.

## What I Don't Know

- Whether `apps/web/app/lib/geotag.ts` / a VE-CCS zone model exists on any *unmerged* branch. On the reviewed branch it does not — which is what matters for costing D8b, but the Board should confirm the dependency rather than trust the "reuse" wording.
- The empirical rate of volunteer device-sharing / impersonation. This is the *only* fact that could move the face path off KILL-unless-justified, and it is unmeasured. Absent that number, the registry solves a threat of unknown size at a known, permanent, population-concentrated cost.
- False-reject rates of any specific face/liveness model on the actual demographics of the Venezuelan volunteer corps and their device fleet. The bias risk is asserted (correctly) but unquantified for this population.
- How HOS-2026-001-08 models a volunteer account and whether it already contemplates device-bound keys — determines how cheap the recommended D8a path actually is.
- The retention/deletion enforcement mechanism for the coarse-location flags (F6): who can query the movement dataset, for how long, and whether coordinators-exempt asymmetry is an explicit, signed-off choice or an accident of the exemption.
