# Principals Review: HOS-2026-008-R1 (addendum, incl. role-scoping)

## My Recommendation

**RESHAPE** — approve the direction, but replace ROLE-scoped geofencing with ACTION-scoped geofencing, and downgrade one honesty claim from "guaranteed" to "best-effort" so the record matches what the mechanism can actually deliver.

Two distinct things are on the table and they do not share a verdict:

- **The on-device-only dedup check** is a genuine principles *improvement* over the killed D1 design. On data-minimization it moves from VIOLATED (a corpus-wide biometric registry) to ALIGNED-with-one-caveat (zero server-side biometric collection). This is not a cosmetic relabel; it is a structural change in what an adversary who compromises the HOS backend obtains: nothing. I ratify the *architecture*. My reservations are about whether one of its load-bearing honesty claims ("never transmitted") is stated as a guarantee the code cannot yet keep.

- **The role-scoping refinement** contains a correct underlying insight wearing the wrong clothes. The insight — "geofencing should attach to physical-presence *assertions*, not to the fact of being a volunteer" — is right and is a principled generalization. But binding that insight to a *self-declared account ROLE* introduces a trust-and-honesty and auditability problem the ACTION-scoped form does not have, and it is strictly less Occam-consistent. I reach the same endpoint the interim orchestrator note did (action-scoping), but by the principles route, not by deferring to it — and I add the part it left implicit: a self-declared role, used to *exempt* someone from a check, is an unverifiable claim doing security-relevant work, which is exactly the pattern this project's principles forbid.

I am **not** re-litigating D1 (KILL, settled), D2 (GREEN_LIGHT device-key+PIN, settled), or the deferred "sensitive operations" step-up. Those are out of scope and I treat them as fixed.

## Principles Alignment

Scored separately for the two pieces, since they diverge.

### Piece A — On-device-only face dedup (registration only)

- **AI recommends, people decide**: **ALIGNED** — the dedup outcome is a local, deterministic "have I seen this face on this device before?" comparison surfaced at registration; it gates nothing life-affecting and (per the accepted risk) a non-biometric fallback lane must exist for anyone who can't complete the capture. No automated, unverified, life-affecting action. Holds *as long as* the check is never wired to hard-block registration with no fallback — same drift risk D3 already has.
- **Information before interfaces**: **NEUTRAL** — the check adds one capture step to onboarding to buy a weak-but-real anti-Sybil signal. It neither advances nor betrays "correct information to the right person." It is a cost paid once, not an information win; that is fine, it just isn't this principle's concern.
- **Trust & honesty**: **CONDITIONAL / VIOLATED-unless-restated.** The problem is the specific wording "NEVER transmitted to or stored by any HOS server," which the proposal itself elevates to "a verifiable engineering invariant … must be testable." Two honesty gaps: (1) a CI network-traffic test can prove *the HOS code paths we wrote* send no image/embedding bytes; it **cannot** prove the OS, the browser, a keyboard/IME, an analytics SDK, or a future backup feature won't move that data — so "never transmitted" is honest only if scoped to "no HOS application code transmits it," not stated as an absolute property of the data's lifetime. (2) The OS-backup-sync risk (named in the proposal's own risks) means "never leaves the device" can silently become false without any HOS code change. Claiming an absolute where only a best-effort, code-scoped guarantee is achievable is the exact class of overclaim this principle exists to catch (the HOS-002-D4 "sent" scar). **Fix the claim and this flips to ALIGNED.** See Logic Check C2.
- **Data minimization / least-PII**: **ALIGNED — and materially stronger than the killed design.** This is the strongest point in the whole addendum. The server collects, exposes, and transmits *zero* biometric data. That is not "less retention," it is "no collection," which is the ceiling this principle asks for. One residual, non-fatal caveat: on a *shared* device, embeddings for multiple people accumulate in one local store, so the minimized-at-the-server win is partially given back at the device (a seized shared phone exposes several people, not one). Minimization therefore holds cleanly at the server boundary and requires an explicit device-cache lifecycle (wipe/rotate per shift) to hold at the device boundary. ALIGNED at the layer that drove the KILL; conditioned at the device layer.
- **Auditability**: **NEUTRAL / mild tension.** Every append-only, attributable *state change* should be logged — but by design the dedup comparison produces **no server-side event at all** (that's the point). So there is a deliberate, defensible auditability gap: HOS cannot audit what it never sees. This is the correct trade (auditability of biometric events would require collecting them, defeating the whole design), but it should be *named* as an accepted exception, not left as an unstated hole. What *is* auditable and *should* be logged: "account X completed registration via on-device dedup vs. via the non-biometric fallback lane" — a boolean, not a biometric. Log that; it's the attributable state change.
- **Crisis-grade & reversible**: **ALIGNED, with the same one-time-friction caveat the parent already accepted.** Reversibility here is actually *better* than a server design: since nothing is stored server-side, there is nothing to leak, subpoena, or fail to delete; a user who clears local storage simply re-registers. The residual cost is the one-time camera/lighting friction at signup (bad camera, injury, darkness), which the mandated non-biometric fallback lane makes recoverable rather than a lockout. Reversible.

**Net (Piece A):** ALIGNED or better on four of six, NEUTRAL on one (auditability, by deliberate design), CONDITIONAL on one (trust-and-honesty, fixable by restating the transmission claim honestly). This is the near-inverse of D1's four-of-six-VIOLATED profile. The architecture is sound; only the *wording of its guarantee* violates a principle.

### Piece B — Geolocation re-scoping

Scored for the ROLE-based form as proposed, then contrasted with the ACTION-based form I recommend.

- **AI recommends, people decide**: **ALIGNED (both forms)** — geolocation stays strictly advisory, never a hard block, per settled D3. Unchanged. The re-scoping doesn't touch this.
- **Information before interfaces**: **NEUTRAL (both)** — scoping *which* submissions carry a proximity signal is an information-routing refinement; it neither adds polish nor withholds needed information.
- **Trust & honesty**: **ROLE form: AT-RISK. ACTION form: ALIGNED.** This is the pivot. Under the ROLE form, a submission from a "remote/translator" account *skips* the location check because of a **self-declared, unverified attribute**. If a field volunteer marks themselves "remote" to dodge the check, the account now honestly-looking carries an exemption it didn't earn, and — worse — a coordinator seeing "no location claim" cannot tell "genuinely remote contributor" from "field volunteer who declared remote to skip the check." That is a claim (this account is remote) doing security-relevant work with nothing behind it. Under the ACTION form, there is *no claim to verify*: a translation edit or text-only contribution has no location field, so there is nothing to exempt and nothing to spoof — the absence of a proximity signal is a true statement about the submission, not an unverified statement about a person. Honesty is a property the ACTION form gets for free and the ROLE form has to *defend*.
- **Data minimization / least-PII**: **ACTION form marginally better.** ACTION-scoping captures location only on submissions that assert a location — the minimum. ROLE-scoping *also* achieves this at capture time, but it requires the account model to *store a role attribute* used for exemption decisions, i.e. one more piece of security-relevant PII-adjacent state per account, plus the machinery to trust it. Slightly more state, for a worse honesty result. Minimization prefers the form that stores less and trusts less.
- **Auditability**: **ROLE form introduces a debt; ACTION form does not.** If a role *exempts* an account from a check, then to be auditable the role assignment itself must be an append-only, attributable event — *who* granted/self-asserted this role, *when*, on what basis. The proposal's role concept is self-service (a hard requirement, to admit remote translators with no coordinator to vouch), so the "attributable" part is "the user asserted it about themselves," which is auditable-but-worthless as a trust signal. ACTION-scoping has nothing to audit here because it makes no role-exemption decision. The append-only-and-attributable principle is satisfied vacuously by the ACTION form and only weakly by the ROLE form.
- **Crisis-grade & reversible**: **ACTION form strictly better on the sometimes-remote/sometimes-field volunteer.** A bilingual helper who mostly translates but occasionally travels to a shelter does not fit a *fixed* account-level role; forcing them into one is brittle in exactly the fluid, reassignment-heavy reality D3 already flagged. ACTION-scoping follows the submission — location-bearing report → proximity signal; text contribution → none — regardless of who the person "is" this week. Reversible and crisis-shaped without a role-change workflow.

**Net (Piece B):** the ROLE form is AT-RISK on trust-and-honesty and creates an auditability debt; the ACTION form is ALIGNED across the board. The *insight* behind the re-scoping is correct; the *implementation vehicle* (a self-declared role used for exemption) is the part that violates principles.

## Logic Check

**C1 — "geofencing should apply only to roles/actions that inherently assert physical presence." — The GENERALIZATION HOLDS; the ROLE half of the "roles/actions" phrasing does not.**
The move from "coordinators are a special-case exemption" to "geofencing attaches to physical-presence *assertions*" is a valid generalization and is more principled than the special-case it replaces — it explains *why* coordinators and remote translators are both exempt (neither asserts field presence) instead of enumerating exemptions ad hoc. So the underlying rule is sound. But the proposal states the rule as "roles/**actions**," conflating two scopings that are not equivalent. A *role* is a persistent, self-declared property of an account; an *action* (submission) is a concrete event that either does or does not carry a location field. "Does this submission assert a location?" is directly observable from the submission. "Is this account exempt?" requires trusting a self-declared attribute. The generalization is real; the correct carrier for it is the ACTION, and the ROLE is a non-sequitur smuggled in alongside it. **Holds as "actions"; does not hold as "roles."**

**C2 — "'never transmitted to or stored by any HOS server' is a verifiable, testable engineering invariant." — HOLDS ONLY IN A NARROWER FORM THAN STATED.**
What a network-request allowlist / CI check can actually verify: *no HOS application code path emits image or embedding bytes to an HOS endpoint.* That is real, worth building, and I endorse it. What it **cannot** verify, and what the sentence as written implies: that the data never leaves the device by *any* means — OS-level cloud backup of IndexedDB (named in the proposal's own risks), a browser sync feature, a future "sync my HOS data" convenience, a third-party SDK. The honest, defensible claim is: **"No HOS server ever receives or stores biometric data, and no HOS application code transmits it; data-at-rest on the device may still be swept into OS/browser backups outside HOS's control, which is an explicit non-goal to enable and a documented residual risk."** Stated that way it is true and testable. Stated as an unqualified "never transmitted," it overclaims — the same failure mode as HOS-002-D4's hard-coded "sent." **The invariant is real; the sentence must be scoped to HOS code, not the data's whole lifetime.**

**C3 — "the dedup check should apply uniformly to every self-registering account regardless of field/remote role, since Sybil-resistance is about identity, not location." — HOLDS, and is internally consistent with C1.**
This is correct and, notably, keeps the two mechanisms cleanly orthogonal: identity-dedup is location-agnostic (applies to everyone), location-proximity is identity-agnostic (applies to location-bearing submissions). The proposal gets this separation right. It is also the tell that confirms C1: the addendum itself treats dedup as attaching to the *act of registering* (an action) and correctly refuses to scope it by role — it should extend the same action-not-role logic to the geofencing half and doesn't. **Holds, and undercuts the ROLE-scoping of the geofence by its own reasoning.**

**C4 — "the self-declared-remote spoofing vector is low-stakes because geolocation is advisory-only." — HOLDS, but does not rescue the ROLE form.**
It is true that since D3 is advisory-and-never-blocking, successfully gaming the role to skip the check only removes a *soft* signal a coordinator was already free to distrust — the blast radius is small. But "the harm from the loophole is small" is not the same as "the loophole is principled." The trust-and-honesty objection (C1, Piece B honesty score) is not primarily about *exploit damage*; it is about the record *misrepresenting* a self-declaration as if it were a checked property, and about a coordinator being unable to distinguish "remote" from "declared-remote-to-skip." Low stakes lowers urgency; it does not make an unverifiable-claim-doing-security-work into good design. **Holds as a severity bound; does not convert the ROLE form to ALIGNED.**

**C5 — "on-device-only changes data-minimization from VIOLATED to ALIGNED vs. the server-side design." — HOLDS at the server boundary; PARTIAL at the device boundary.**
Server-side: unambiguously true — zero collection beats minimized retention. Device-side: a shared device accumulates multiple embeddings, so the *system as a whole* still concentrates several people's biometric data in one seizable place, just a much smaller place than a corpus-wide registry. The verdict-changing claim (server gets nothing) holds; the completeness of "minimized everywhere" requires the shared-device cache lifecycle to be defined. **Holds for the verdict; conditioned for completeness.**

## Simplest Solution

**Occam's razor prefers ACTION-scoped geofencing over ROLE-scoped, and it is not close — because the simpler form deletes an entire problem rather than solving it.**

The end the re-scoping serves: *don't attach a physical-presence signal to submissions that make no physical-presence claim.* The minimum mechanism that achieves that end: **check the submission for a location field.** If it has one, compute the advisory proximity flag (per settled D3); if it doesn't, there is nothing to check. That's it. No role attribute, no role-assignment workflow, no "how do we trust a self-declared role" question, no auditability debt for role grants, no ambiguity between "remote" and "declared-remote," and it handles the sometimes-remote/sometimes-field volunteer with zero special-casing because the check follows the submission, not the person.

The ROLE form, by contrast, requires: (a) a role concept in the account model — which **does not exist yet** (verified: `08-real-auth-and-roles.yaml` lists `roles (coordinator / agency-admin / responder)` as **TODO**, and crucially the *specific* role the addendum needs — "remote/translator/moderator" — is **not even in that planned enumeration**, so this is not "ride the coming role model," it is "expand the coming role model's taxonomy"); (b) a decision about who can assert/grant that role (self-service, per the hard requirement); (c) an audit trail for role assignment to keep it attributable; and (d) the pairing the interim note correctly identified — a remote-role account must be *technically unable* to file a location-bearing report, or the exemption is a pure loophole. That is a lot of machinery to answer a question ("does this submission assert a location?") that the submission already answers by itself.

**The tradeoff, stated honestly:** the ACTION form gives up the ability to express, at the account level, "this person is a remote contributor" as a first-class identity fact. If HOS later wants that concept for *other* reasons (e.g. routing, permissions on who may edit translations), it may still build a role model — but geofencing-exemption should **not** be the justification for it, and geofencing should not *depend* on it. Scope the check to the action now; if a role model arrives later for independent reasons, the action-scoped geofence keeps working unchanged and never has to trust the role. That is the Occam path: solve the geofencing question with the thing that already carries the answer (the submission), and don't make a soft advisory signal a customer of an unbuilt, self-declared trust attribute.

For **Piece A**, the simplest *honest* form is the architecture as proposed with exactly one edit: replace the absolute "never transmitted" with the code-scoped claim from C2, add the CI network-allowlist test that actually backs it, log the boolean "registered via dedup vs. fallback" (the auditable state change), and define the shared-device cache lifecycle. No architectural change — the design is already minimal; only its guarantee needs to be sized to what's provable.

## Technical Debt Created

- **Role-model-dependency debt (Piece B, ROLE form) — larger than the parent's already-flagged dependency, and mis-sized if taken.** The parent review already noted D2/role work rides on the in-progress `HOS-2026-001-08`. But that task's planned role set is `coordinator / agency-admin / responder` — **the "remote/translator/moderator" role this addendum needs is not in it.** So ROLE-scoping doesn't just wait on the existing foundation; it *expands the foundation's scope* (a new role category, self-service assignment, and its audit trail) to serve a soft advisory signal. **The ACTION form owes none of this** — it depends only on "does this submission have a location field," which is a property of the intake payload, not the account model. Choosing ACTION-scoping *retires* this debt line entirely. **Owned?** The proposal names the role-model-doesn't-exist-yet dependency in its risks, but under-sizes it: it reads as "reuse the coming role model," when the truth is "add a role the coming model doesn't currently plan."
- **Honesty-overclaim debt (Piece A) — currently unacknowledged as a *claim* problem.** "Never transmitted" as an absolute is prose that the CI test cannot fully back (C2). Left as written, the gap between the stated guarantee and the provable guarantee is a standing honesty liability of exactly the HOS-002-D4 species. **Owned?** The proposal owns the *engineering* invariant ("must be testable") but not the *scope* of the claim — it doesn't flag that "no HOS code transmits" ≠ "the data never leaves the device."
- **Auditability-gap debt (Piece A) — deliberate, but should be documented as an accepted exception.** By design the dedup comparison emits no server event, so it is unauditable by construction. This is the *correct* trade, but an undocumented "we have no audit trail for the biometric step" invites a future reviewer to treat it as an oversight and "fix" it by logging embeddings server-side — silently resurrecting D1. **Owned?** No — needs an explicit line: "the dedup comparison is intentionally not audited; only the dedup-vs-fallback boolean is logged; server-side logging of embeddings is forbidden (would recreate the killed registry)."
- **Shared-device cache-lifecycle debt (Piece A) — named, not yet resolved.** Multiple embeddings accumulating on one shift-shared device is a data-minimization erosion at the device boundary (C5). **Owned?** Named in the proposal's risks; resolution (wipe/rotate per shift vs. accept-and-document) deferred. Acceptable to defer, but must be decided before ship, not after.
- **Principle-drift debt (both, inherited) — the same enforced-invariant requirement D3 already carries.** "Advisory, never a block" (geofence) and "fallback lane always available" (dedup) are both enforced today only by intent; both need a test that fails if the check ever hard-blocks, per the D3 precedent. **Owned?** The geofence half is covered by settled D3's conditions; the *dedup* half (no-hard-block-without-fallback) should inherit the identical invariant and doesn't yet say so explicitly.

## Confidence Score

**0.82.**

High because the two load-bearing findings are checkable facts, not taste: (1) I verified the role model the ROLE-scoping depends on does not exist and — the sharper point — that the specific "remote/translator" role is not even in the planned taxonomy (`tasks/backlog/HOS-2026-001-ai-matching/08-real-auth-and-roles.yaml`, `roles (…) TODO`, enumerating only coordinator/agency-admin/responder); and (2) I confirmed there is still zero geolocation/haversine code in the tree and intake location is still free-text (`apps/web/app/lib/services/intake.ts:39,81`), so the geofence remains net-new and the "does this submission assert a location?" test is a property of the intake payload, exactly where ACTION-scoping needs it. The ACTION-over-ROLE recommendation then follows directly from four of the project's own six principles (trust-and-honesty, data-minimization, auditability, crisis-grade-and-reversible all prefer the form that stores less and trusts no self-declaration) — so it is the project's logic applied, not my preference. I land where the interim orchestrator note landed on action-scoping, but I reached it independently via the principles and add the auditability/verifiability argument it left implicit; I did not defer to it.

Not higher because: (a) I cannot confirm from inside this repo whether a browser-deployable face-embedding model is actually feasible on the documented low-end Android fleet — that is the addendum's load-bearing *feasibility* unknown and it is the Researcher's question, not mine, but if it fails, Piece A is moot regardless of principles; (b) face-embedding *invertibility* is genuinely unconfirmed either way, so my data-minimization ALIGNED for Piece A rests on "the server stores nothing" (unarguable) rather than on "an embedding is safe even if it leaks" (unverified) — the verdict holds on the former alone, but the completeness of the safety story depends on the latter; (c) I did not audit the qclock prior art (it is an external project, correctly not in this repo — `grep qclock` returns nothing outside the decision log), so I take no position on whether the 1-2 week port estimate is right, which is again the Researcher's lane.

## What I Don't Know

- Whether a browser-deployable (WASM/TF.js-class) face-embedding model actually runs acceptably on the documented low-end Android fleet. If it doesn't, Piece A's principles-alignment is irrelevant — the check can't be performed. This is the addendum's stated load-bearing unknown and I defer it to the Researcher.
- Whether face embeddings are practically invertible / fingerprint-like if a copy ever leaked (e.g. via OS backup). My Piece A data-minimization verdict is safe on the "server collects nothing" ground regardless; but whether "it's just an embedding, not a photo" is a *complete* safety argument depends on this, and it is unconfirmed.
- Whether HOS actually wants an account-level "remote contributor" concept for *independent* reasons (routing, edit-permissions on translations). If it does, a role model may be justified on those grounds — but that does not change my finding that geofencing-exemption must not be the justification for it, nor depend on it.
- The real duplicate-account threat model: is the harm confusion/redundant-reporting (which the weak on-device dedup adequately addresses) or a determined actor running coordinated fake identities (which it trivially does not)? The proposal accepts the weaker guarantee explicitly; whether that acceptance is correct depends on this unmeasured threat rate, which is the Contrarian's lane.
- The shared-device cache lifecycle decision (wipe/rotate per shift vs. accept-and-document the multi-person accumulation) — deferred in the proposal, must be resolved before ship for the device-boundary minimization to hold.
