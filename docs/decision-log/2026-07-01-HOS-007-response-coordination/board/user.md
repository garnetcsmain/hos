# User Review: HOS-2026-007

## My Recommendation
🟡 RESHAPE

## User Scenario
I am **Yoli, a shelter coordinator** in Maiquetía. It's day 3. I have 40 cots, 34
are full, I'm out of infant formula and low on clean water, and my phone is on 1
bar over a shared hotspot. Right now I manage this in three WhatsApp groups and a
notebook. In the next 10 minutes I need two things: tell the world "I can take a
few more people and I urgently need formula + water," and find out if anyone is
actually bringing it — without typing my exact address into something a stranger
can read.

Second persona: **Marcos, a logistics lead at a responder org.** He has a truck of
water and 6 hours. He needs to see the open needs by area and claim the ones he
can serve, so two orgs don't both drive to the same shelter while another gets nothing.

## Does This Solve It?
- Pain addressed: **PARTIAL** — the shelter-capacity + needs/supplies board is
  exactly the shared picture Yoli and Marcos lack today. But only if it's faster
  to update than WhatsApp and doesn't expose Yoli's location.
- Would I use it: **MAYBE** — yes if entering a need is 3 taps and works offline;
  no if it's a heavy form, or if I fear the board tells the wrong people where the
  minors are.

## Friction Points
1. Slow/rich entry under stress → I'll stay on WhatsApp. Needs a dead-simple,
   offline-tolerant "post a need" (category + quantity + urgency + district), nothing more.
2. "Fulfilled" with no proof → if the board says water arrived and it didn't, I
   plan around water I don't have. I need to be the one who confirms it arrived,
   not the org that claims it. (Same lesson as the family-notification fix.)
3. Precise location exposed → I will not publish my shelter's exact address.
   District-level only, and only to authenticated responders — never a public page.
4. Stale entries → if I can't tell a need is 2 days old, I don't trust any of it.
   Show me how fresh each item is.

## Missing Piece
A **claim-and-confirm loop I can trust**: when Marcos claims my need, I want to see
"claimed by [org]", and I decide when it flips to "received" — because I'm the one
who knows if the water is actually in my hands. Without that, this is just a
prettier WhatsApp that can also be read by people I'm hiding from.

Also missing for me as a family user: nothing here helps *me* directly yet, and
that's fine — but don't let coordination distract from finishing the part that
tells me whether my son has been found. That's still the thing I refresh at 3am.

## Confidence Score
0.8 — I know Yoli's and Marcos's day well and the thin board maps to it. Not higher
because whether real coordinators adopt it over WhatsApp depends entirely on entry
speed and trust, which I can't judge until I hold it.
